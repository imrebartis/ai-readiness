00Introduction

A diagnostic, not a pitch.

This assessment is a diagnostic, not a pitch. It's designed to answer one question: what can AI realistically do for your operations in the next 90 days, and what needs to be true for it to work?

Score high and you'll know what kind of engagement makes sense. Score low and you'll know exactly where to start, and what to fix before committing to anything larger.

Time
Approximately 10 minutes end to end.
Instructions
For each question, pick the option that most honestly describes where you are today, not where you'd like to be.
Output
Six dimension scores, one total, and a band you can act on.

Your band →
0 – 25
Start with one bounded use case.

You're not ready for integrated AI systems, but you are ready for a single well-scoped automation that produces visible value in 2–4 weeks. The goal at this stage isn't to transform operations. It's to build one proof point your team can see and trust.

Concrete starting points at this level:

  • Meeting notes → structured follow-ups and action items
  • Inbound email triage and drafting
  • Document summarization for specific recurring workflows
  • Standardized responses to frequent customer questions
AvoidBuying platform tooling, running multiple pilots in parallel, or committing to ambitious integrations before the first small win lands.
Your band →
26 – 51
Ready for targeted pilots.

You have enough foundation to run focused AI work in one or two areas with measurable outcomes. The leverage here is in picking the right starting point: repeatable process, accessible data, clear stakeholder, and instrumenting it well enough to know whether it worked.

AvoidBroad “AI strategy” engagements. They're slower and less convincing than one pilot that ships and shows numbers.
Your band →
52 – 75
Ready for systematic adoption.

You have the foundations to build AI into operations as an integrated layer, not as a collection of standalone tools. At this level the question shifts from “does AI help?” to “which sequence of integrations compounds fastest?”

Engagements at this level typically look like:

  • End-to-end workflow integration across two or more systems
  • Internal copilots grounded in your own operational data, with guardrails and audit trails
  • Decision-support systems tied to KPIs you already report on
  • Governance, logging, and rollback patterns defensible to auditors or regulators
AvoidTreating readiness as a green light to parallelize pilots. The leverage here is sequencing and integration, not breadth — one compounding build beats three standalone ones.

§Closing

A note on honesty.

This assessment is both a diagnostic and a qualifier, and I'd rather be upfront about that than pretend it's neutral.

If you scored in the top band, you're likely a good fit for the work I do, and the next conversation should reflect that.

If you scored in the bottom band, the highest-leverage thing right now probably isn't a consulting engagement, it's one small, scoped win. If you reach out, that's what I'll tell you, and I'll point you at resources to run it yourself if that's the better path.

The goal is to be useful to you regardless of which side of that line you're on.
